1. Change: Genitive

Change the following phrases into the genitive case, while retaining the same number (singular or plural).

  1. vir sapiens

  2. res acres bellas

  3. ignes lignei et mutabiles

  5. carmina brevia et pecuniam magnam

  6. lacus longos

  7. cursus gloriosus

  8. capita misera atrocia

    Your answer:
    Correct answer: carminum brevium et pecuniae magnae

  6. lacus longos
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: lacuum longorum

  7. cursus gloriosus
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: cursus gloriosi

  8. capita misera atrocia
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: capitum miserorum atrocium

  9. domus sempiterna
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: domus sempiternae or domi sepiternae

  10. genera audacia
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: generum audacium

3. Uses of the Genitive

In each sentence, determine the use of the genitive -- possessive, price, charge/penalty, material, or quality -- then translate.

  1. illa est femina magnae virtutis. <{illa} virtus, -tutis III = “virtue”>


  2. habeo domum ligni.


  3. illum hominem magni aestimant.


  4. carmen Vergilii canam! <{ille} Vergilius, -i II = “Vergil”>


  5. lex nova parvi est. <novus, -a, -um = “new”; parvus, -a, -um = “little”>


  6. iudex viros maiestatis damnat. <{ille} iudex, -icis III = “judge”; {illa} maiestas, -tatis III = “treason”>


  7. homo maximae prudentiae esse possum. <maximus, -a, -um = “greatest”; {illa} prudentia, -ae I = “wisdom”>


  8. filium Ciceronis amare debeo. <{ille} Cicero, -onis III = “Cicero”>


  9. videre ovum auri cupio. <{illud} ovum, -i II = “egg”>


  10. illam adulterii accusat. <{illud} adulterium, -i II = “adultery”; accuso, -are = “to accuse”>

    Possible translations: That is a woman of great virtue.

4. More Uses of the Genitive

In each sentence, determine the use of the genitive -- partitive, remembering/forgetting, pity, subjective, objective, or predicative -- then translate. Note that there may be multiple genitives in the sentence.

  1. illud poema pretiosum est poetae.


  2. matris amor est absolutus. <absolutus, -a, -um = “unconditional”>


  3. immemores sumus animalium et agrorum. <immemor, -moris = “forgetful”; {ille} ager, agri II = “field”>


  4. pars mei animi patriam regere cupit. <{illa} patria, -ae I = “fatherland, country”>


  5. me miseret filiorum.


  6. bonorum hominum est arma deponere et pacem dissertare. <{illa} pax, pacis III = “peace”; disserto, -are = “to discuss”>


  7. magnus numerus feminarum videt vitalem cursum esse brevem. <{ille} numerus, -i II = “number”>


  8. Cicero miseriam rei publicae habet. <res publica = “the Republic”>


  9. memoria gloriae homines timidos movere debet. <moveo, -ere = “to move”>


  10. patriae amor admonet virum officiorum. <{illud} officium, -i II = “duty”>

responsa ostende!
  1. illud poema pretiosum est poetae.
    Your answer: unset
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Correct answer: Predicative
    Possible translations: That expensive poem is the poet's.

  2. matris amor est absolutus.
    Your answer: unset
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Correct answer: Subjective or Objective
    Possible translations: A mother's love is unconditional; love for a mother is unconditional.

  3. immemores sumus animalium et agrorum.
    Your answer: unset
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here

5. Uses of the Subjunctive

In each sentence, determine the use of the subjunctive -- potential, hortatory, deliberative, or optative -- then translate.

  1. forsitan rex atrox iram deponat. <{illa} ira, -ae I = “anger”>


  2. admoneamne matrem periculi gravis? <gravis, -e = “serious”>


  3. poemata gloriae scribamus.


  4. utinam filii et filiae ne parentes timeant. <{ille/illa} parens, -entis III = “parent”; timeo, -ere = “to fear”>


  5. dei ament homines.


  6. dicas sapientem poetam amores deorum canere.


  7. utinam homines deos ament.


  8. deponate formidinem mortis! <{illa} formido, -dinis III = “fear”; {illa} mors, mortis III = “death”>


  9. non credas magnam Fortunam miseros regere.


  10. libidone pecuniae non satiet animos sapientium? <{illa} libido, -dinis III = “lust”>

  1. forsitan rex atrox iram deponat.
    Your answer: unset
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Correct answer: Potential
    Possible translations: Perhaps the fierce king may set aside his anger.

  2. admoneamne matrem periculi gravis?
    Your answer: unset

    Correct answer: Deliberative

  3. poemata gloriae scribamus.
