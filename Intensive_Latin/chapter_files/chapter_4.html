1. Change: Passive

Change the following verb forms into the passive voice, while retaining the same person (1st, 2nd, or 3rd), number (singular or plural), and mood (indicative or subjunctive).

  1. narrat

  2. damno

  3. quaeris

  4. faciat

  5. detis

  6. admoneam

  7. audit

  8. canatis

  9. aestimemus

  10. negas
responsa ostende!
  1. narrat
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: narratur

  2. damno
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: damnor

  3. quaeris
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: quaereris, quaerere

  4. faciat
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: faciatur

  5. detis
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: demini

  6. admoneam
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: admonear

  7. audit
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: auditur

  8. canatis
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: canamini

  9. aestimemus
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: aestimemur

  10. negas
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: negaris, negare

3. Translate: Active or Passive Verbs

Translate the bolded verbal phrase in English in the following sentences into a Latin verb form. All responses should be one-word answers.

  1. The poem is sung by the poet.

  2. The stories are told by the women.

  3. The boy eats his vegetables in order to be strong. <valeo, -ere = “to be strong”>

  4. They want to know whether we love wisdom.

  5. Good citizens follow the rules of the city.

  6. They dress up in order that they be seen.

  7. I am unsure whether I should follow.

  8. The soldiers are warned about the present danger.

  9. You die with the knowledge that your family will survive you.

  10. The priestess asks from which gate they are setting out.
responsa ostende!
  1. The poem is sung by the poet.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: canitur

  2. The stories are told by the women.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: narrantur

  3. The boy eats his vegetables in order to be strong.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: valeat

  4. They want to know whether we love wisdom.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: amemus

  5. Good citizens follow the rules of the city.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: sequuntur

  6. They dress up in order that they be seen.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: videantur

  7. I am unsure whether I should follow.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: sequar

  8. The soldiers are warned about the present danger.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: monentur

  9. You die with the knowledge that your family will survive you.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: moreris, morere

  10. The priestess asks from which gate they are setting out.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: proficiscantur

4. Translate

Translate the following sentences into idiomatic English.

  1. omnis veritas magni aestimatur.

  2. philosophi est sequi virtutis cursum et vitare vitii viam. <vito, -are = “to shun”; {illa} via, -ae = “way, path”>

  3. rex bonus dona populo dat ut obliviscatur irae. <{illa} ira, -ae = “anger”>

  4. audax vir facit bona facta ut fabulae gloriae narrentur. <{illud} factum, -i = “deed”>

  5. rogatur num oves parvae sint pulchrae.

  6. utinam amor deorum cupiatur!

  7. femina quaerit num carmina Vergilii canantur.

  8. Caesaris legiones negant hostes proficisci. <{illa} legio, -ionis = “legion”; {ille} hostis, -is = “enemy”>

  9. forsitan Senecae vita scribatur ne memoria philosophi moriatur. <{ille} Seneca, -ae = “Seneca”>

  10. puer fabulas audire cupit ut vitam sapientis viri vivat.
responsa ostende!
  1. omnis veritas magni aestimatur.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: All truth is considered of great value.

  2. philosophi est sequi virtutis cursum et vitare vitii viam.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: It is (the task) of a philosopher to follow the course of virtue and avoid the path of vice.

  3. rex bonus dona populo dat ut obliviscatur irae.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: The good king gives gifts to the people so that they [i.e., the people] forget their anger.

  4. audax vir facit bona facta ut fabulae gloriae narrentur.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: The bold man does good deeds so that stories of glory be told.

  5. rogatur num oves parvae sint pulchrae.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: It is asked whether the small sheep are beautiful.

  6. utinam amor deorum cupiatur!
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: If only the love of the gods may be desired!

  7. femina quaerit num carmina Vergilii canantur.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: The woman asks whether the songs of Vergil should be sung; the woman asks whether the songs of Vergil are being sung.

  8. Caesaris legiones negant hostes proficisci.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: Caesar's legions deny that the enemies are setting out.

  9. forsitan Senecae vita scribatur ne memoria philosophi moriatur.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: Perhaps the life of Seneca may be written down so that the memory of the philosopher not die.

  10. puer fabulas audire cupit ut vitam sapientis viri vivat.
    Your translation: student-translation_appears_here
    Possible translations: The boy wants to hear stories so that he live the life of a wise man.
