2. Translate: Verbs

Translate the bolded English verb phrase into Latin.

  1. He was asking whether she had received the letter.

  2. I sang the song about Aeneas and the Trojans.

  3. You all had heard that the Gauls are entering Rome.

  4. Were the slaves being harmed by the foreman?

  5. The poems were taught by the teacher.

  6. Since the civil war, swords have not been held in the temple.

  7. It was mentioned that the girl was setting out for home at that time.

  8. Caesar says that the enemy destroyed their camp the night before.

  9. You had thought that his father was a lawyer.

  10. I used to be called a slave, but now I'm a freedman.
responsa ostende!
  1. He was asking whether she had received the letter.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: rogabat

  2. I sang the song about Aeneas and the Trojans.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: cecini, canebam

  3. You all had heard that the Gauls are entering Rome.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: audiveratis

  4. Were the slaves being harmed by the foreman?
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: nocebantur

  5. The poems were taught by the teacher.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: docta sunt, docebantur

  6. Since the civil war, swords have not been held in the temple.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: habiti sunt

  7. It was mentioned that the girl was setting out for home at that time.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: proficisci

  8. Caesar says that the enemy destroyed their camp the night before.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: perdidisse

  9. You had thought that his father was a lawyer.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: cogitaveras

  10. I used to be called a slave, but now I'm a freedman.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: appellabar

  1. utinam deis placeremus.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Optative
    Correct translation: If only we had pleased the gods.

  2. forsitan aves domum in hoc arbore facerent.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Potential
    Correct translation: Perhaps the birds might have made a home in this tree.

  3. rex illam feminam ne cuperet.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Jussive
    Correct translation: The king ought not have desired that woman.

  4. poeta sapiens de agri cultura cecinit ut doceret hos audientes.
    Your answer:

4. Translate

Translate each sentence into Latin. Keep in mind that you may not need to translate every word from English into Latin in order to provide a satisfactory translation.

  1. I do not remember whether the consul had said that. <{ille} consul, -ulis = “consul”>

  2. Our friends might have thought that they were worthy of praise (but they probably weren't). <{illa} laus, laudis = “praise”>

  3. If only she hadn't died (but she definitely had).

  4. Don't put aside the love of your country.

  5. The men desired to learn the laws in order that they might recognize injustices. <{illa} iniuria, -ae = “injustice”>
responsa ostende!
  1. I do not remember whether the consul had said that.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: non memini num consul id dixisset.

  2. Our friends might have thought that they were worthy of praise (but they probably weren't).
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: forsitan nostri amici cogitarent digni laude esse.

  3. If only she hadn't died (but she definitely had).
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: utinam ne moreretur.

  4. Don't put aside the love of your country.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: ne deposueris / deposueritis patriae amorem.

  5. The men desired to learn the laws in order that they might recognize injustices.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: viri leges discere cupiverunt / cupivere ut iniurias cognoscerent.
