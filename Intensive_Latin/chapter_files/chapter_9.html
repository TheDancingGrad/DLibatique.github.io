  3. homini <admonitus, -a, -um>

  4. manuum <nocens, -entis>

  5. corpore <repens, -entis (repo, -ere = “to crawl”)>

4. Translate: Imperative

Translate the following commands into Latin using the imperative mood.

  1. Give me your hand.

  2. Seize the women and the children.

  3. Don't read these letters.

  4. Persuade the men to come to the city.

  5. Please the gods, and don't harm humankind.

  6. Believe the words of poets.

  7. Bring your faith and your cares.

  8. Make me dinner. <{illa} cena, -ae = “dinner”>

  9. Tell me your thoughts. <{illa} sententia, -ae = “thought, opinion”>

  10. Lead me to your general. <{ille} imperator, -oris = “general, commander”>
responsa ostende!
  1. Give me your hand.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: da mihi tuam manum.

  2. Seize the women and the children.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: capi / capite feminas et filios.

  3. Don't read these letters.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: noli / nolite legere has litteras; ne legeris / legeritis has litteras.

  4. Persuade the men to come to the city.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: persuade / persuadete viris ut veniant ad urbem.

  5. Please the gods, and don't harm humankind.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: place / placete deis, et noli / nolite nocere hominibus; ne nocueris / nocueritis hominibus.

  6. Believe the words of poets.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: crede / credite verbis poetarum.

  7. Bring your faith and your cares.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: fer / ferte tuum / vestrum fidem et curas.

  8. Make me dinner.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: fac / facite mihi cenam.

  9. Tell me your thoughts.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: dic / dicite mihi tuas / vestras sententias.

  10. Lead me to your general.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: duc / ducite me ad tuum / vestrum imperatorem.

5. Translate

Translate the following sentences into idiomatic English.

  1. litteris acceptis Cicero in Senatum venit.

  2. matre haec hortante filii celeriter suos labores fecere. <celer, -eris, -ere = “swift”>

  3. consul ita effecit ut populus Romanus eum magni aestimaret. <Romanus, -a, -um = “Roman”>

  4. Caesare duce Pompeius iram in populo incitavit. <incito, -are = “to incite”>

  5. adulescens puellam tam animose amavit ut ex urbe profectus esset et solus leonem interfecisset.

  6. tanta ira Caesar captus est ut legionem misisset quae trans Rubiconem contenderet. <{illa} legio, -ionis = “legion”; {ille} Rubico, -onis = “Rubicon (River)”; contendo, -ere = “to hasten”>

  7. nuntiis ab rege Gallorum interfectis alii legati recta ad portam magna celeritate cucurrerunt. <{illi} Galli, -orum = “the Gauls”; {ille} legatus, -i = “ambassador”; {illa} porta, -ae = “gate”; {illa} celeritas, -tatis = “swiftness”; curro, -ere, cucurri, cursum = “to run”>

  8. mulier Sabina tam sincere dixit ut Romani et Sabini foedus amicitiae sanctae sancirent. <Sabinus, -a, -um = “Sabine”; sancio, -ire, sanxi, sanctum = “to consecrate”>

  9. hortare viros ne quibus mulieribus filiisve noceant.

  10. aliis aliter persuasis noli in certum periculum te conferre. <aliter = “otherwise”; confero, -ferre, -tuli, -latum + refl. pron. = “to take oneself”>
responsa ostende!
  1. litteris acceptis Cicero in Senatum venit.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: After the letters were accepted, Cicero came into the Senate.

  2. matre haec hortante filii celeriter suos labores fecere.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: While the mother was exhorting these things, the sons swiftly did their tasks.

  3. consul ita effecit ut populus Romanus eum magni aestimaret.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: The consul brought it about that the Roman people judge him to be of great value.

  4. Caesare duce Pompeius iram in populo incitavit.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: When Caesar was leader, Pompey incited anger in the people.

  5. adulescens puellam tam animose amavit ut ex urbe profectus esset et solus leonem interfecisset.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The youth loved the girl so spiritedly that he had set out from the city and killed a lion alone.

  6. tanta ira Caesar captus est ut legionem misisset quae trans Rubiconem contenderet.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: Caesar was seized by such great anger that he sent a legion to hasten across the Rubicon.

  7. nuntiis ab rege Gallorum interfectis alii legati recta ad portam magna celeritate cucurrerunt.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: After the messengers were killed by the king of the Gauls, the other ambassadors ran directly to the gate with great speed.

  8. mulier Sabina tam sincere dixit ut Romani et Sabini foedus amicitiae sanctae sancirent.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: The Sabine woman spoke so sincerely that the Romans and Sabines consecrated a treaty of holy friendship.

  9. hortare viros ne quibus mulieribus filiisve noceant.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: Exhort the men not to harm any women or children.

  10. aliis aliter persuasis noli in certum periculum te conferre.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Although the others are otherwise persuaded, don't take yourself into certain danger.
