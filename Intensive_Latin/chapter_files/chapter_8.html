3. Translate: More Verbs

Give the Latin equivalent for the phrase(s) in bold.

  1. If he will have returned to Rome, he will have been made consul.

  2. If Fulvia should tell Cicero of the conspiracy, she would deserve praise.

  3. If I were you (but I'm not), I would learn the laws (but I won't).

  4. If the kings had ruled with dignity (but they didn't), the people would have desired to worship them like gods (but they didn't).

  5. When Cato heard the news, he rushed to the Curia.

  6. While Catiline was writing his letter, Cicero was making plans to protect the city.

  7. Caesar gave a great speech in the Senate, because he perceived the danger of his colleague's suggestion.

  8. Although his gifts please the gods, nevertheless he angers those who accuse him of treachery.

  9. He said that he would come to the festivities shortly.

  10. The woman told me that her sons were being killed at home.

  11. They will be asked whether they knew the consequences of their actions.

  12. He said that the men who were destroying the republic would be brought to justice.

  13. If the soldiers will lay down their arms, the war will cease.

  14. I was tired, when yesterday I set out for my daily walk.

  15. A man interrupted me, while I was setting out for my daily walk.
responsa ostende!
  1. If he will have returned to Rome, he will have been made consul.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: factus erit

  2. If Fulvia should tell Cicero of the conspiracy, she would deserve praise.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: dicat, mereat/mereatur

  3. If I were you (but I'm not), I would learn the laws (but I won't).
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: essem, discerem

  4. If the kings had ruled with dignity (but they didn't), the people would have desired to worship them like gods (but they didn't).
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: rexissent, cupivisset

  5. When Cato heard the news, he rushed to the Curia.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: cum Cato audivit

  6. While Catiline was writing his letter, Cicero was making plans to protect the city.
    Your answer:
    Possible answers: cum Catilina scriberet

  7. Caesar gave a great speech in the Senate, because he perceived the danger of his colleague's suggestion.
    Your answer:
    Possible answers: cum conspiceret

  8. Although his gifts please the gods, nevertheless he angers those who accuse him of treachery.
    Your answer:
    Possible answers: cum placeant

  9. He said that he would come to the festivities shortly.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: venturum esse

  10. The woman told me that her sons were being killed at home.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: interfici

  11. They will be asked whether they knew the consequences of their actions.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: rogabuntur / quaerentur, sciverint

  12. He said that the men who were destroying the republic would be brought to justice.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: perderent, latum iri

  13. If the soldiers will lay down their arms, the war will cease.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: deponent

  14. I was tired, when yesterday I set out for my daily walk.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: cum profectus/a sum

  15. A man interrupted me, while I was setting out for my daily walk.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: cum proficiscerer

4. Translate

Translate the following sentences.

  1. ego vinum bibam, si tu hoc cibum edes. <{ille} cibus, -i = “food”>

  2. dux dixit se in urbem legiones qui hostes vincerent misurum esse. <vinco, -ere, vici, victus = “to conquer, defeat”>

  3. alii in urbe remanere debent, cum alii in agris bellum gerant.

  4. si quis sit qui mihi credat, ego ei meam fidem dem. <{illa} fides, -is = “trust”>

  5. cum ille ab omnibus magni aestimatus sit, mihi tamen non placet. <tamen = “nevertheless”>

  6. illa rogabitur num sui patris invidia toleretur. <tolero, -are = “to tolerate”>

  7. scimus nostros filios mores veterum discituros esse. <{ille} mos, moris = “way, habit”; vetus, -eris = “old, ancient”>

  8. quaerentur num amicitia quam inter se iunxerint satis valeat. <iungere amicitiam = “to join into friendship”>

  9. cum consul abesset, Clodius movit populum qui eum in exilium mitteret. <absum, abesse = “to be away”; {illud} exilium, -i = “exile”; moveo, -ere, movi, motus = “to move, provoke”>

  10. si ulla femina ad urbem ut carmen audiat venerit, id canere voluero.
responsa ostende!
  1. ego vinum bibam, si tu hoc cibum edes.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: I will drink the wine, if you will eat this food.

  2. dux dixit se in urbem legiones qui hostes vincerent misurum esse.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: The leader said that he would send into the city legions who would conquer / to conquer the enemy.

  3. alii in urbe remanere debent, cum alii in agris bellum gerant.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: Some ought to remain in the city, while others wage war in the fields.

  4. si quis sit qui mihi credat, ego ei meam fidem dem.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: If there should be anyone who believes me, I would give my trust to him/her.

  5. cum ille ab omnibus magni aestimatus sit, mihi tamen non placet.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Although he has been considered of great value by all, nevertheless he does not please me.

  6. illa rogabitur num sui patris invidia toleretur.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: She will be asked whether the hatred of her own father is / will be tolerated.

  7. scimus nostros filios mores veterum discituros esse.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: We know that our sons will learn the ways of the ancients.

  8. quaerentur num amicitia quam inter se iunxerint satis valeat.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: They will be asked whether the friendship which joined them is / will be strong enough.

  9. cum consul abesset, Clodius movit populum qui eum in exilium mitteret.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: While the consul was away, Clodius provoked the people to send him into exile.

  10. si ulla femina ad urbem ut carmen audiat venerit, id canere voluero.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: If any woman will have come to the city to hear a song, I will have wanted to sing it.
