4. Infinitive

Identify which of the following infinitive constructions are present in the sentence: impersonal statement, complementary infinitive, or oblique statement; keep in mind that more than one may be present. Then translate.

  1. oportet filium docere.


  2. mater dicit viros sapientiam amare.


  3. reges cupiunt regere.


  4. viri cogitant homines regere deos. <cogitant = “they think”>


  5. dei possunt regere homines.


  6. non decet virum remanere. <decet = “it is proper;” remanere = “to stay behind”>


  7. vir solet dicere sententiam. <solet = “he/she/it is accustomed”; sententia, sententiam I = “thought, opinion”>


  8. femina cupit virum videre manus.


  9. homines non queunt audire Fortunam. <queunt = “they can”>


  10. pater videt filios discere leges. <discere = “to learn”>


  11. licet amare deos. <licet = “it is permitted”>


  12. puer cupit puellam videre.


  13. placet discere leges. <placet = “it seems good”>


  14. audent vitam amare. <audent = “they dare”>


  15. viri sciunt deos homines regere semper cupere. <sciunt = “they know”>

responsa ostende!

    Your answer:

    Correct answer: Impersonal Statement
    Possible translations: It is fitting to teach a son; it is fitting that a son teach.
