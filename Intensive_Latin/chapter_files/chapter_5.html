1. Change: Dative and Ablative

Change the following noun and adjective phrase into the case and number indicated.

  1. difficilis lingua <dative singular>

  2. nox magna <ablative plural>

  3. vultus totus <dative singular>

  4. cornu forte <dative plural>

  5. littera sapiens <dative singular>

  6. nomen acre <ablative singular>

  7. periculum mutabile <ablative singular>

  8. primus amor <dative plural>

  9. cursus magnus <dative singular>

  10. genus dulce <ablative plural>
responsa ostende!
  1. difficilis lingua <dative singular>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: difficili linguae

  2. nox magna <ablative plural>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: noctibus magnis

  3. vultus totus <dative singular>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: vultui toti

  4. cornu forte <dative plural>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: cornibus fortibus

  5. littera sapiens <dative singular>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: litterae sapienti

  6. nomen acre <ablative singular>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: nomine acri

  7. periculum mutabile <ablative singular>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: periculo mutabili

  8. primus amor <dative plural>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: primis amoribus

  9. cursus magnus <dative singular>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: cursui magno

  10. genus dulce <ablative plural>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: generibus dulcibus

2. Match: Case Uses

The following is a chart of 20 case uses and 20 sentences. Each sentence showcases one of the uses in the chart with the word in bold type. Match each sentence with its case use by typing in the corresponding letter. You can use the checkboxes to keep track of which ones you have used.

a. genitive of material b. genitive of price c. ablative of material d. accusative of goal
e. accusative of direct object f. ablative of manner g. ablative of time when h. dative of indirect object
i. ablative of agent j. ablative of price k. ablative of accompaniment l. ablative of place in which
m. genitive of quality n. double dative o. ablative of separation p. ablative of means
q. predicative genitive r. accusative of duration s. dative of possession t. genitive of possession

  1. ille mihi donum dat.

  2. ille mihi donum dat.

  3. feminae illum ex urbe sequuntur.

  4. vir illud parvo pretio vendit. <vendo, -ere = “to sell”>

  5. mihi est amicus.

  6. ego illum parvi aestimo.

  7. ad agros proficiscamur.

  8. philosophus a rege interficitur.

  9. facio statuam e saxo.

  10. tibi est ovum auri. <{illud} ovum, -i = “egg”>

  11. ille auxilio urbi est. <{illud} auxilium, -i = “help”>

  12. filii totam noctem dormiunt. <dormio, -ire = “to sleep”>

  13. ille stilus est Vergilii.

  14. stilus Vergilii est fortis.

  15. cum amicis ludo. <ludo, -ere = “to play”>

  16. illa appellatur femina magnae virtutis.

  17. cum magna celeritate sequitur. <{illa} celeritas, -tatis = “swiftness, speed”>

  18. rex civem gladio interficit. <{ille or illa} civis, -is = “citizen”>

  19. nos in silva sumus.

  20. illo die viri ab urbe proficiscuntur.

    Your answer:
    Correct answer: b. genitive of price

  7. ad agros proficiscamur.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: d. accusative of goal

  8. philosophus a rege interficitur.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: i. ablative of agent

  9. facio statuam e saxo.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: c. ablative of material

  10. tibi est ovum auri.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: a. genitive of material

  11. ille auxilio urbi est.
    Your answer:

  12. filii totam noctem dormiunt.

  13. ille stilus est Vergilii.

responsa ostende!
  1. ille mihi donum dat.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: He gives me a gift; he gives a gift to me.

  2. ille mihi donum dat.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: He gives me a gift; he gives a gift to me.

  3. feminae illum ex urbe sequuntur.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The women follow him out of the city.

  4. vir illud parvo pretio vendit.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The man sells it for a small price.

  5. mihi est amicus.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: I have a friend.

  6. ego illum parvi aestimo.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: I judge him of little value.

  7. ad agros proficiscamur.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Let us set out to the fields.

  8. philosophus a rege interficitur.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The philosopher is killed by the king.

  9. facio statuam e saxo.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: I am making a statue out of stone.

  10. tibi est ovum auri.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: You have an egg (made) of gold.

  11. ille auxilio urbi est.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: He is a source of help to the city.

  12. filii totam noctem dormiunt.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The sons sleep the whole night long.

  13. ille stilus est Vergilii.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: That pen is Vergil's.

  14. stilus Vergilii est fortis.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Vergil's pen is brave.

  15. cum amicis ludo.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: I am playing with friends.

  16. illa appellatur femina magnae virtutis.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: She is called a woman of great virtue.

  17. cum magna celeritate sequitur.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: With great speed, he/she/it follows.

  18. rex civem gladio interficit.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The king kills the citizen with a sword.

  19. nos in silva sumus.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: We are in the woods.

  20. illo die viri ab urbe proficiscuntur.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: On that day the men set out from the city.
