5. Translate

Translate the following sentences into English.

  1. poeta carmina potentia canere primus incipit.

  2. mutabilis est amor.

  3. servi soli satiare patronos avaros cupitis. <{ille} servus, -um II = “slave”; {ille} patronus, -um II = “patron”>

  4. oportet carmina esse brevia.

  5. acris puella viros miseros cornua atrocia non audire videt.

  6. poetae audaces canunt daemones neque fortunam neque sapientiam amare.

  7. leges non bonas scribis et pueros iuvenes puellasque timidas poemata brevia canere non doces. <iuvenis, -e = “young”>

  8. boni discere artes bellas cupimus. <bellus, -a, -um = “beautiful”>

  9. res varias avarae capiunt. <capio, -ere = “to seize”>

  10. arma virumque cano. <{illa} arma, arma (pl.) II = “arms, weapons”>
responsa ostende!
  1. poeta carmina potentia canere primus incipit.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The poet begins to sing powerful songs first.

  2. mutabilis est amor.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Changeable is love.

  3. servi soli satiare patronos avaros cupitis.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: You slaves alone want to satisfy the greedy patrons.

  4. oportet carmina esse brevia.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: It is proper that songs be short.

  5. acris puella viros miseros cornua atrocia non audire videt.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The keen girl sees that the miserable men do not hear the fierce horns.

  6. poetae audaces canunt daemones neque fortunam neque sapientiam amare.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: The bold poets sing that the spirits love neither changeable fortune nor wisdom.

  7. leges non bonas scribis et pueros iuvenes puellasque timidas poemata brevia canere non doces.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: You write laws that are not good, and you do not teach young boys and fearful girls to sing short poems.

  8. boni discere artes bellas cupimus.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: We good people want to learn beautiful skills.

  9. res varias avarae capiunt.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Greedy women seize various things.

  10. arma virumque cano.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: I sing of arms and a man.
