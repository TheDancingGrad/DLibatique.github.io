1. Identify: Degree

Identify the degree of the bolded adjective or adverb (positive, comparative, or superlative), and then translate.

  1. ille cupidior potestatis suae quam libertatis populi erat.


  2. hostium duces illos hortati sunt ut luce fortissima proficiscerentur. <{illa} lux, lucis = “light”>


  3. bonum vinum patrem tuum bibere sinam si paulum mihi relicturum esse promittet. <paulum = “a little”>


  4. filiorum omnium in hac familia, ille minime in agris laboravit.


  5. avis in manu digna duabus in frutice est; una enim melior quam nulla est. <{ille} frutex, fruticis = “bush”>


  6. dubitabam num quis umquam fuisset qui elegantius illo dixisset.


  7. cum servi sumptuosissimam cenam praeparavissent, tamen eis a domino non licitum est partem edere. <{ille} hospes, hospitis = “guest”; sumptuosus, -a, -um = “extravagant”>


  8. dicite sapienter, aut nolite dicere aliquid.


  9. pueri aliquid litteris difficilius a magistro doctum iri cognovere.


  10. tam paucas horas dormiebam ut quid hodie non facere possem.


responsa ostende!
  1. ille cupidior potestatis suae quam libertatis populi erat.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Comparative
    Correct translation: That man was more desirous of his own power than the people's freedom.

  2. hostium duces illos hortati sunt ut luce fortissima proficiscerentur.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Superlative
    Correct translation: The leaders of the enemy exhorted them to set out when the light was strongest.

  3. bonum vinum patrem tuum bibere sinam si paulum mihi relicturum esse promiserit.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Positive
    Correct translation: I will allow your father to drink the good wine if he promises that he will leave a little for me.

  4. filiorum omnium in hac familia, ille minime in agris laboravit.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Superlative
    Correct translation: Of all the sons of this family, he has worked the least in the fields.

  5. in manu avis digna in frutice duabus est; una enim quam nihil melior est.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Comparative
    Correct translation: A bird in the hand is worth two in the bush, for one is better than nothing.

  6. dubitabam num quis umquam fuisset qui elegantius illo dixisset.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Comparative
    Correct translation: I was wondering whether there had ever been anyone who had spoken more elegantly than he.

  7. cum servi sumptuosissimam cenam praeparavissent, tamen illis a domino non licitum est partem edere.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Superlative
    Correct translation: Although the slaves had prepared the most extravagant dinner, nevertheless it was not allowed by the master that they eat a part.

  8. dicite sapienter, aut nolite dicere aliquid.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Positive
    Correct translation: Speak wisely, or do not say anything.

  9. pueri aliquid litteris difficilius a magistro doctum iri cognovere.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Comparative
    Correct translation: The boys recognized that something more difficult than letters was going to be taught by the teacher.

  10. tam paucas horas dormiebam ut quid hodie non facere possem.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Positive
    Correct translation: I slept for so few hours that I was not able to do anything today.

2. Change: Degree

Change the bolded adjectives or adverbs in the following phrases or sentences into the degree indicated while maintaining the same gender, number, and case (if applicable). Give the changed adjective or adverb as your answer.

  1. miserrimarum matrum <positive>

  2. saepius quam ego edis. <superlative>

  3. simillimos reges <comparative>

  4. consilium magis iucundum <positive>

  5. libertas maximi populi <comparative>

  6. diu vivebat. <superlative>

  7. pleniorum arborum <positive>

  8. puella dulcius canens <superlative>

  9. vitio deformi <comparative>

  10. coniugi magnopere credo. <comparative>
responsa ostende!
  1. miserrimarum matrum <positive>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: miserarum

  2. saepius quam ego edis. <superlative>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: saepissime

  3. simillimos reges <comparative>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: similiores

  4. consilium magis iucundum <positive>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: iucundum

  5. libertas maximi populi <comparative>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: maioris

  6. diu vivebat. <superlative>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: diutissime

  7. pleniorum arborum <positive>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: plenarum

  8. puella dulcius canens <superlative>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: dulcissime

  9. vitio deformi <comparative>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: deformiori

  10. coniugi magnopere credo. <comparative>
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: magis

3. Dependent Subjunctive Clauses

For the following English sentences, indicate whether the subordinate clause should be introduced by ut, ne, ut non, or quin if translated into Latin.

  1. I am afraid that I may fall.

  2. You were afraid that he would not arrive in time.

  3. He is not so optimistic that he does not expect difficulties.

  4. She is feeding the dogs so that they may not continue to whine.

  5. They are so confident about winning the game that they are not practicing.

  6. He introduced this concept in an earlier chapter in order to prepare the students for more difficult concepts later on.

  7. Your patron is afraid that in a few months, he may not have enough money to give to his clients on a daily basis.

  8. He chopped down the tree to have a better view of his fields.

  9. She has so little rhythm that she does not dance in public.

  10. I am afraid that she may dance at the banquet and make a fool out of herself.
responsa ostende!
  1. I am afraid that I may fall.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ne

  2. You were afraid that he would not arrive in time.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ut

  3. He is not so optimistic that he does not expect difficulties.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: quin

  4. She is feeding the dogs so that they may not continue to whine.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ne

  5. They are so confident about winning the game that they are not practicing.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ut non

  6. He introduced this concept in an earlier chapter in order to prepare the students for more difficult concepts later on.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ut

  7. Your patron is afraid that in a few months, he may not have enough money to give to his clients on a daily basis.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ut

  8. He chopped down the tree to have a better view of his fields.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ut

  9. She has so little rhythm that she does not dance in public.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ut non

  10. I am afraid that she may dance at the banquet and make a fool out of herself.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ne

4. Translate

Translate the following sentences into idiomatic English.

  1. timebam ne difficilius iter deligeres.

  2. rex ad agros latus est ut exitium quod ab hostibus effectum esset videret.

  3. litteris a discipulis intellectis magister dubitabat num pueros numeros, quos nondum didicissent, docere aut dare examen de litteris oporteret. <nondum = “not yet”>

  4. Cicero dixit Catilinam interficere omnes cives Romanos voluisse, et populares et optimates, sed maxime Ciceronem ipsum.

  5. plus pecuniae habuerat priusquam filii geniti sunt. <priusquam = “before”>

  6. cum plurimos honores mereret, invidiam omnium aliorum qui regi placuerant incitavit.

  7. ne veritus sis ut veniam cum huc me voces.

  8. pueri qui ad templum prima luce iere tam clamosi quam petulantes erant. <clamosus, -a, -um = “noisy”; petulans, -ntis = “bad-tempered”>

  9. unum amicum sincerum habere malo quam multos adulatores blandos. <{ille} adulator, -oris = “flatterer”; blandus, -a, -um = “fawning”>

  10. maiori salutati civitati nostrae ille nuntius fuisset nisi timuisset verius loqui. <nisi = “if ... not”>
responsa ostende!
  1. timebam ne difficilius iter deligeres.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: I was afraid that you would choose the more difficult journey.

  2. rex ad agros latus est ut exitium quod ab hostibus effectum esset videret.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: The king was brought to the fields to see the destruction which had been brought abotu by the enemies.

  3. litteris a discipulis intellectis magister dubitabat num pueros numeros, quos nondum didicissent, docere aut dare examen de litteris oporteret.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: After letters had been learned by the students, the teacher wondered whether he ought to teach the boys numbers, which they had not yet learned, or give a test about letters.

  4. Cicero dixit Catilinam interficere omnes cives Romanos voluisse, et populares et optimates, sed maxime Ciceronem ipsum.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: Cicero said that Catiline wanted to kill all the Roman citizens, both the populares and the optimates, but most of all Cicero himself.

  5. plus pecuniae habuerat priusquam filii geniti sunt.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: He had had more money before his sons were born.

  6. cum plurimos honores mereret, invidiam omnium aliorum qui regi placuerant incitavit.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: Because he earned the most honors, he stirred up the envy of all the others who had pleased the king.

  7. ne veritus sis ut veniam cum huc me voces.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: Do not fear that I may not come when you call me here.

  8. pueri qui ad templum prima luce iere tam clamosi quam petulantes erant.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: The boys who went to the temple at first light were as noisy as [they were] bad-tempered.

  9. unum amicum sincerum habere malo quam multos adulatores blandos.
    Your translation:
    Correct translation: I prefer to have one true friend than many fawning flatterers.

  10. maiori salutati civitati nostrae ille nuntius fuisset nisi timuisset verius loqui.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: That messenger would have been a greater source of safety for our city if he had not been afraid to speak more truthfully.
