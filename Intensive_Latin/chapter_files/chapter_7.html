1. Identify: Pronouns and Adjectives

Identify each emboldened word as one of the following: relative pronoun, interrogative pronoun, interrogative adjective, indefinite pronoun, or indefinite adjective.

  1. mihi est filius quem corde omni amo.

  2. quem librum in bibliotheca mea vidisti? <{illa} bibliotheca, -ae = “library”>

  3. quaesivit num qua femina esset quae carmen audivisset.

  4. rogabatur num quis esset qui carmen audivisset.

  5. a quibus litterae missae sunt et quibus sunt?

  6. quod bellum gestum est in Gallia?

  7. ille enim est qui vitas aliorum ante suam ponat.

  8. dubitabam num quibus daemonibus illo die placuissem. <dubito, -are = “to be unsure, doubt”>

  9. quas dixisti Caesarem deas appellare cupisse?

  10. ei dixere, si quis fuisset qui laude dignus esset, se eum laudavisse.
responsa ostende!

    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Relative Pronoun

  2. quem librum in bibliotheca mea vidisti?
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Interrogative Adjective

  3. quaesivit num qua femina esset quae carmen audivisset.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Indefinite Adjective

  4. rogabatur num quis esset qui carmen audivisset.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Indefinite Pronoun

  5. a quibus litterae missae sunt et quibus sunt?
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Interrogative Pronoun

  6. quod bellum gestum est in Gallia?
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Interrogative Adjective

  7. ille enim est qui vitas aliorum ante suam ponat.
    Your answer:
    Correct answers: Relative Pronoun

  8. dubitabam num quibus daemonibus illo die placuissem.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Indefinite Adjective

  9. quas dixisti Caesarem deas appellare cupisse?
    Your answer:

    Your answer:
    Correct answer: Indefinite Pronoun

  1. ille dixit pulchram feminam quam magni aestimavisset quoque magnae sapientiae fuisse.
    Your answer:

    Correct answer: Relative Clause in Indirect Discourse
    Possible translation(s): He said that the beautiful woman whom he had considered of great value had also been of great wisdom.

  2. quis est qui multa bona rei publicae gerat?
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Relative Clause of (General) Characteristic
    Possible translation(s): Who is there who would bring many good things to the republic?

  3. si eum gravis periculi admonuisset, id non fecisset.

    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Contrary to Fact Condition (Past)
    Possible translation(s): If he had reminded him of the serious danger, he would not have done it.

    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Relative Clause of Purpose

  5. si tecum ibi essem, tibi oscula darem.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Contrary to Fact Condition (Present)
    Possible translation(s): If I were there with you, I would give you kisses.

  6. pecunia mihi data est qua domum emam.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Relative Clause of Purpose
    Possible translation(s): Money, with which I am to buy a home, has been given to me.

  7. nemo fuit qui virtutem in summo honore haberet.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Relative Clause of (General) Characteristic
    Possible translation(s): There was no one who would hold virtue in the highest honor.

  8. si idem genus irae in populo non fuisset, Cicero orationes suas eo modo scribere non voluisset.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Contrary to Fact Condition (Past)
    Possible translation(s): If the same kind of anger had not been in the people, Cicero would not have wanted to write his own speeches in that way.

  9. is vir dicit Caesarem ipsum, qui nuper ad castra venisset, orationem apud milites habuisse.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Relative Clause in Indirect Discourse
    Possible translation(s): This man says that Caesar himself, who had come recently to the camp, gave a speech to the soldiers.

  10. si multos varios cives in concordiam inter se non ferrem, civitatem gerere consul non possem.
    Your answer:
    Your translation:
    Correct answer: Contrary to Fact Condition (Present)
    Possible translation(s): If I were not to bring many different citizens into harmony amongst themselves, I would not be able to manage the city as consul.

3. Translate: Pronouns and Adjectives

Give the Latin equivalent for the phrases in bold.

  1. The ancients wrote that they received their stories from their forefathers.

  2. The mother gave her own life for the security of her children.

  3. These soldiers said that Caesar himself led their forces across the Rubicon.

  4. Cicero believed that the memory of himself would be of use to future Romans.

  5. Catiline was sure that his co-conspirators fostered the same lust for power. <{illa} lubido, -dinis = “lust”>

  6. Give me your hand!

  7. I want your courage in battle.

  8. The consul declared that they themselves should carry the burden of debt.

  9. I shall write in order, to the best of my ability, to make the memory of us the greatest possible.

  10. Cato argued that he must decide what would be best for himself.
responsa ostende!
  1. The ancients wrote that they received their stories from their forefathers.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: se

  2. The mother gave her own life for the security of her children.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: suam

  3. These soldiers said that Caesar himself led their forces across the Rubicon.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ipsum

  4. Cicero believed that the memory of himself would be of use to future Romans.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: sui

  5. Catiline was sure that his co-conspirators fostered the same lust for power.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: eandem

  6. Give me your hand!
    Your answer:
    Possible answers: tuam, vestram

  7. I want your courage in battle.
    Your answer:
    Possible answers: tuam, vestram

  8. The consul declared that they themselves should carry the burden of debt.
    Your answer:
    Possible answers: eos ipsos, illos ipsos

  9. I shall write in order, to the best of my ability, to make the memory of us the greatest possible.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: nostri

  10. Cato argued that he must decide what would be best for himself.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: sibi

4. Sequence of Tenses

With each sentence, change the main course verb from primary to secondary tense, or vice versa; then make any other necessary changes to the rest of the sentence (i.e., subordinate clause verbs). Give the whole sentence as your answer.

  1. imperator dicit se nuntios ad hostium castra mittere qui sua mandata regi transmittant. <{ille} imperator, -oris = “commander”; transmitto, -ere = “to hand over, carry over”>

  2. femina quaesivit num suus filius ex urbe proficisceretur ut ad se veniret.

  3. audimus illos viros feminas quae sapientiam magni aestiment cupere.

  4. dicit se ipsam viam sequi qua ad virtutem veniat.

  5. ego dico virum qui hanc cenam praeparaverit meum servum olim fuisse, sed propter eius bonam fidem me eum libertum nunc facere. <praeparo, -are = “to prepare”; {illa} fides, -is = “trust, faith”; {ille} libertus, -i = “freedman”>
responsa ostende!
  1. imperator dicit se nuntios ad hostium castra mittere qui sua mandata regi transmittant.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: imperator dicebat/dixit se nuntios ad hostium castra mittere qui sua mandata regi transmitterent.

  2. femina quaesivit num suus filius ex urbe proficisceretur ut ad se veniret.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: femina quaerit num suus filius ex urbe proficiscatur ut ad se veniat.

  3. audimus illos viros feminas quae sapientiam magni aestiment cupere.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: audiebamus/audivimus illos viros feminas quae sapientiam magni aestimarent cupere.

  4. dicit se ipsam viam sequi qua ad virtutem veniat.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: dicebat/dixit se ipsam viam sequi qua ad virtutem veniret.

  5. ego dico virum qui hanc cenam praeparaverit meum servum olim fuisse, sed propter eius bonam fidem me eum libertum nunc facere.
    Your answer:
    Correct answer: ego dixi virum qui hanc cenam praeparavisset meum servum olim fuisse, sed propter eius bonam fidem me eum libertum nunc facere.
